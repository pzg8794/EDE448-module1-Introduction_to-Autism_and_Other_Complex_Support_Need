This journal argues that developmental difference is simultaneously neurological, lived, relational, cultural, historical, and socially interpreted. Each section uses my lived experience and K--5 technology teaching placement to interpret the required Module 1 sources within the broader course conversation. The sources do not always agree, and that disagreement matters: diagnostic and educator resources can open access, while critical, historical, neurodiversity, and lived-experience sources warn against allowing categories, interventions, or success narratives to erase the person.
